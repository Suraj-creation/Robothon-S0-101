1Install LeRobotsoftware

The [feetech] extra installs the Feetech SDK needed to communicate with STS3215 servos. This is the only Python install you need for the hardware layer.

!CRITICAL: Know your motor gear ratios (Leader ≠ Follower)critical

The leader and follower arms use DIFFERENT motors. This is the most commonly missed detail.

AxisLeader gear ratioFollower gear ratio
Base / Shoulder Pan (J1)1/191 (faster)1/345 (stronger)
Shoulder Lift (J2)1/3451/345
Elbow Flex (J3)1/1911/345
Wrist Flex (J4)1/1471/345
Wrist Roll (J5)1/1471/345
Gripper (J6)1/1471/345

The leader uses lighter-ratio motors (1/147, 1/191) so you can move it easily by hand. The follower uses heavy 1/345 gearing for torque and holding power.

Never swap motors between arms. Installing a 1/345 motor on the leader makes it very hard to backdriven manually. Installing a 1/147 motor on the follower makes it weak and unable to hold its own weight.
2Find USB ports for each armdo once

Connect the follower arm USB. Run:

lerobot-find-port

Follow the prompt — it will ask you to disconnect the USB while running to identify which port belongs to which arm. Note the port string (e.g. /dev/ttyUSB0 on Linux).

Repeat with the leader arm USB connected.

Label your USB cables with tape ("LEADER" / "FOLLOWER"). On Linux, ttyUSB numbers can swap between reboots if both are plugged in simultaneously.
!Set motor IDs and baudrates — ONE MOTOR AT A TIMEcritical

All new motors ship with ID=1. You must set unique IDs before daisy-chaining. The official script walks you through each motor individually.

For the follower arm:

lerobot-setup-motors \
    --robot.type=so101_follower \
    --robot.port=/dev/ttyUSB1   # your follower port

The script will say: "Connect the controller board to the 'gripper' motor only and press enter." Connect ONLY that motor, press Enter. It auto-sets ID=6 for gripper. Then it asks for wrist_roll (ID=5), and so on up to shoulder_pan (ID=1).

At each step: only ONE motor connected to the board. Do NOT daisy-chain yet. Check power supply and USB are connected before pressing Enter.

For the leader arm:

lerobot-setup-motors \
    --teleop.type=so101_leader \
    --teleop.port=/dev/ttyUSB0   # your leader port

Same process. Motor IDs are set 6→5→4→3→2→1 (gripper first, shoulder pan last).

Once all motors are set, daisy-chain the 3-pin cables: shoulder_pan → shoulder_lift → elbow → wrist_flex → wrist_roll → gripper. Connect shoulder_pan to the controller board.

!Calibrate both armscritical

Calibration maps physical joint angles to encoder counts. Without it, the leader and follower will not be in sync, and any trained policy will not transfer between robots.

Follower calibration:

lerobot-calibrate \
    --robot.type=so101_follower \
    --robot.port=/dev/ttyUSB1 \
    --robot.id=my_follower_arm

Step 1: Move the arm to the middle of all joint ranges (the "rest" pose). Press Enter.
Step 2: Move each joint through its full range of motion — all the way to one end, then all the way to the other. The calibration records the min/max counts.

Leader calibration:

lerobot-calibrate \
    --teleop.type=so101_leader \
    --teleop.port=/dev/ttyUSB0 \
    --teleop.id=my_leader_arm
After both calibrations succeed, your robot is physically ready. The calibration file is saved in ~/.cache/lerobot/calibration/ and is reused for all future sessions.

!Power supply: leader and follower are DIFFERENTcritical
The two arms use different voltages. Swapping them will damage the servos.
ArmRequired Power SupplyWhy
Leader arm5V / 6ALower-ratio motors need less voltage; 12V would overstress them
Follower arm12V / 8AHigh-torque 1/345 motors rated for 12V @ 30 kg·cm
Your quickstart card explicitly states: "Leader Arm: Connect to 5V/6A power supply. Follower Arm: Connect to 12V/8A power supply." Double-check this before powering on.
!Do NOT manually modify servo parameterscritical

Your card says: "It is recommended not to manually modify servo parameters until you are thoroughly familiar with the process."

This means: do NOT use the Feetech servo configuration tool (SCSCL) to change PID gains, speed limits, or deadband settings unless you know exactly what you are doing. The motors are pre-configured at the factory for SO-101 operation.

The lerobot-setup-motors command only sets ID and baudrate — those are safe to change. Everything else: leave at factory defaults until you are fully operational.
!Calibration direction matters — watch Servo #3 (Elbow)critical

Your card says: "Incorrect calibration directions may cause servos to move toward mechanical limits, potentially causing overload or servo damage, particularly Servo #3 on the follower arm."

During calibration, when the script asks you to move joints through their range:

Move Servo #3 (Elbow Flex) in the CORRECT direction as shown in the calibration video. Moving it the wrong way during calibration causes the follower to immediately slam into a limit stop on first teleoperation — this can break the physical structure or burn out the motor.

Watch the official calibration video before doing this step: youtube.com/watch?v=70GuJf2jbYk (timestamp 1:06:32 shows baud rate, configuration, and calibration).

3Maximum payload is 400 gramshardware

Your card states: "The recommended maximum payload for this robotic arm is 400 grams."

For your pick-and-place task, keep the pick object under 400g. The STS3215 at 1/345 gearing provides 30 kg·cm stall torque, but extended load above 400g at full arm extension will trigger overload protection (80% stall, 2s timeout) and eventually burn out motors.

ObjectOK?
Small cube, bottle cap, light box (<200g)Safe for long-duration testing
Medium object 200–400gSafe, but avoid extended hold at full extension
Over 400gDo not attempt — servo damage risk

"Servo damages caused by improper operation (such as overload or burnout) are not covered by the warranty."

This directly means: if you skip calibration and run the policy, if you exceed 400g payload, or if you connect the wrong power supply, any servo damage is out-of-pocket. Follow the steps in order.
